Elbrus from the North - Eastern Top

Elbrus is the highest point in Europe, which by its nature is a long extinct stratovolcano. The mountain has two peaks (5642 and 5621 meters), separated by a saddle. We will climb to the lower, eastern peak. Big the area of the slopes is under the glaciers. Therefore, to climb Elbrus from the north, you must have a good physical training, basic skills of using an ice ax and crampons, movement in a bundle are welcomed.

If you do not have such experience yet, then in the first days of our program there will be an opportunity to learn the basics of technology high-altitude mountaineering. A couple of extra days have been added to the route for better acclimatization and in case bad weather. After all, these are two of the most important factors in climbing such difficulty.

  • Possible without experience

  • Overnight in tents

  • Up to 10 people in the group
